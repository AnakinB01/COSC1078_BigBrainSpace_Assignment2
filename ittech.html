Autonomous Vehicles

What does it do?

Autonomous vehicles are more commonly known as self driving cars or driverless cars where the cars can drive itself from one location to another location without human involvement. The vehicles have installed sensors, radars, GPS, machine learning and various amounts of technologies that allow auto driving (Wikipedia 2021). Autonomous cars work by creating and maintaining a map of their surroundings based on the variety of sensors located in different parts of the vehicle, the sensors also detect nearby vehicles and sensors located in the wheels are used to detect the road and other vehicles when parking. Video cameras are used to detect traffic lights, road signs, track other vehicles, and look for pedestrians (Synopsys 2021). “Lidar (light detection and ranging) sensors bounce pulses of light off the car’s surroundings to measure distances, detect road edges, and identify lane markings” (Synopsys 2021, page 1) The software then processes all this information and input, creates a path, and sends instructions to the car’s actuators, which controls the acceleration, braking, and steering. “Encoded rules, obstacle avoidance algorithms, predictive modelling, and object recognition help the software follow traffic rules and navigate obstacles” (Synopsys 2021, page 1). Currently, autonomous vehicles are made available to the public with companies such as Tesla, Google, manufacturing them and making them available however autonomous vehicles are still expensive and are not perfect which requires further development, where in the future the technology will be more refined and be made more available for the general public. Current cost wise, an autonomous vehicle such as a Tesla for example compared to a fuel powered car is more expensive to purchase, charging stations are also an issue as they are uncommon compared to petrol stations where many people will consider the traditional fuel cars instead of electric powered autonomous cars. Currently the technology of autonomous vehicles allows cars to drive from one location to another on autopilot mode however self driving cars are limited due to the Government regulations and that it still requires human manual control whereas, in the future, companies aim for vehicles to be fully autonomous and by teaching them the “edge cases they’ll encounter on the road” (Vox recode 2020). Due to cost wise and limitations, autonomous transport is therefore used and tested more for large companies and countries. However, autonomous transportation is currently being used and is fully functional in some countries. Such as autonomous buses or automated shuttles being tested in several cities worldwide (ScienceDirect), especially recently Singapore has released their first commercial autonomous bus service that runs between two routes. In Hong Kong an automated, fully driverless transportation or “Automated People Mover” is located within Hong Kong International Airport (Wikipedia 2021), it is fully automated and it transports people from separate terminals. With the development with other new and improved technology it will further make autonomous vehicles more efficient, with more sophisticated artificial intelligence being created, better sensors to help detect the environment, and by having improved network capabilities such as the 5G network it allows higher bandwidth where cars will be able to “communicate” with each other and to be able to find traffic signals, better more efficient routes and optimising traffic patterns (Vox recode 2020). Even though the development of autonomous vehicles is beneficial and efficient, it also creates ethical challenges or the “Moral Machine Dilemma” (Valiente Mott, page 1). An autonomous vehicle is unable to make judgments between two critical outcomes, similar to the trolley problem for example, choosing between turning left and hitting a roadblock injuring passengers or turning right and hitting pedestrians. Researchers have been thinking “should autonomous vehicles perhaps adopt utilitarian principles when choosing who lives and who dies?” (Digitalist 2019, page 1). Artificial Intelligence could be taught ethical decisions however people argue that consumers should have the power to decide, therefore autonomous vehicles won’t be perfect.

What is the likely impact?

The development of autonomous vehicles will impact society's lifestyles by changing our ways of travelling, reducing traffic accidents and creating a more eco friendly environment. By having self-driving vehicles, it can benefit traffic efficiency and reduce time consumption on the road. This development will aim to reduce traffic accidents and improve safety as stated by the NRMA that - “94% of accidents are caused by human errors” (NRMA, page 1) and can provide greater mobility options that benefit those with a disability or elderly (NRMA) by allowing them to be able to drive a car without affecting those around them. With sensors and cameras installed on the vehicle, it will ensure the car will drive within the speed limit, this will prevent people from speeding and also contribute towards the prevention of traffic accidents. Autonomous vehicles will reduce traffic congestion which means will drop “60% in harmful emissions” (Thales 2021, page 1). Autonomous vehicles will provide a more environmentally friendly option as self-driving cars such as Tesla are powered using electricity instead of petrol or diesel created from fossil fuels where this change will reduce carbon dioxide emissions. However, potential issues are created with the development of autonomous vehicles as well. There will be a dramatic job loss problem where by having self-driving vehicles, it will replace the current jobs of those who depend on driving, such as public transport drivers, food delivery, those that rely on driving for a job (Valiente Mott). Autonomous vehicles also raise concerns with machine malfunction and error and security issues, even though autonomous vehicles will reduce accidents however “it does not eliminate the risk of accidents caused by machine error” (Valiente Mott, page 1). A small malfunction or security issue can cause major problems and danger towards people, traffic can be manipulated, autonomous cars can be hacked and can change the speed of a car (Valiente Mott).

How will it affect you?

The development of autonomous vehicles will greatly benefit me and the general public as well. The creation of autonomous vehicles can reduce time travelling to and from work, it can also allow the increase of car sharing which would reduce traffic congestion, which can benefit family members and people to reduce time spent on the road. Autonomous vehicles will allow elderly people and those with driving impairments in families to be able to travel efficiently without worrying about not being able to know how to drive a car and affecting those around them (NRMA), and for the people that can drive, parking can be a long process trying to find a convenient spot however, with the development of autonomous vehicles people can arrive outside the location and let the vehicle find a parking and later on pick you up. With self parking you also don’t have to worry about parking tickets as the car will find a parking spot autonomously and change parking after the time limit. It can increase the safety of families and people by having sensors and emergency brake functions that are able to detect crashes, traffic accidents with a faster reaction time than humans which can also decrease the possibility for human error. With the sensors and cameras installed, people also don’t have to worry about speeding. In an autonomous vehicle, people will no longer need to keep an eye out for changing speed limits as the self-driving vehicle will be able to gain information and adjust the speed automatically (2025ad).

Blockchain & Cyrptocurrency

What does it do?

Blockchain is a system of recording information in a way which makes it difficult to change, hack or cheat the system. A blockchain is essentially a digital ledger of transaction, which runs across a massive peer to peer network. Each block in the chain contains a number of transactions, and every time a new transaction occurs on the blockchain, the record of that is added to every participant’s ledger. Using this technology, users can confirm transactions without the need of a central clearing authority. A cryptocurrency is a digital currency that is secured by cryptography, which makes it nearly impossible to fake or over spend. Many cryptocurrencies are decentralized meaning that they have no middle man or central authority controlling the transactions. It is based on a network of blockchain technology-making it a distributed ledger imposed by different devices on a network. There are various cryptocurrencies, but the most common ones are bitcoin and ethereum. Bitcoin is stored on a digital wallet in a phone or computer. In terms of transaction, one person can transfer bitcoins from their wallet to another wallet, and this process is recorded on a public blockchain. While bitcoin is more like a ledger, Ethereum has a more flexible blockchain. While it does documentation and secure transactions from wallet to wallet like bitcoin. Developers can use the ethereum blockchain to make a variety of tools from software to games (which include spending, lending and trading), which exist on a Decentralised Finance (DeFi) application. Decentralised finance is a blockchain based form of finance, where no central authority exists.

What is the likely impact?

There are various benefits of the blockchain system and cryptocurrency. For example, blockchain will allow for faster transactions between two parties. Currently most of the services that facilitate payments take a couple of days for the transaction to complete, and this time frame could increase even further if you are making transactions overseas. This is where blockchain comes into play, with the blockchain technology you can send money without the need to wait for days for the completion of the transaction. Another advantage is product provenance, the blockchain works with the supply chain. Any product we buy has to go through a series of destinations before we get the product, which increases the chance of tampering and fraud with the product. With the help of blockchain usage on the supply chain solves all of these problems. Through blockchain the companies can keep track of the condition of the product, and if the product is tampered with then the system will get alerted and it will discard from the supply chain, thus helping consumers receive the product they paid for, increasing the authenticity of products. Cryptocurrency which runs using the blockchain technology, also has benefits in the currency exchange market. The first being that it’s value isn’t affected by inflation. A problem with the fiat currency is that governments and central banks can manipulate interest rates and issue bonds, which increase the supply of the money, resulting in the decrease of purchasing power of anyone holding the currency. However unlike fiat currencies, cryptocurrency offers a fixed creation schedule and a maximum supply limit. Therefore having a currency which can’t be diluted, helping people maintain purchasing powers. Another benefit is foreign exchange, currently with the central bank (RBA) issued currency is artificially restricted to certain geographical locations, but with cryptocurrency that isn’t the case, it is a border- free currency. However while there are so many positives about blockchain technology and cryptocurrency, there are also negative aspects. Blockchain technology is immutable, that means that once the data has been entered in the system, it cannot be deleted and this could be a big problem arising in terms of privacy. Also immutable networks should only be present if the nodes within the network are equally distributed, but with blockchain technology if an entity owns 50% or more of the nodes, then they can control the network which can make it vulnerable for others. A disadvantage of cryptocurrency, would be that currently it isn’t regulated or backed by government laws. This would mean that it can be exploited by criminals to scam unaware investors, who are looking to invest in cryptocurrency.

How will this affect you?

Blockchain and cryptocurrency will have major impacts on an individual's life. It plays a role in the protection of the customer purchasing items online, this is because blockchain technology records everything that occurs within the supply chain, which means that when a fraud or damage to the product occurs, company’s systems are notified and the product is sent back. This way consumers receive the exact product they bought, which will increase the level of authenticity in the market. The blockchain system shifts the control of some interaction activities from central authorities to the users, which improves transparency. For a common man today, sending money overseas can be troubling process with the fluctuation of fiat currencies and then the banks holding the payment for a few days before transferring the money overseas via the SWIFT system, but with blockchain and cryptocurrency, this process becomes easier and faster with the payment being made and account being settled within 24-48 hours, with no central authority controlling the transaction. Also if you are travelling to a country who has a higher exchange rate compared to Australia, when you get your dollars exchanged for the local currency you might not get enough money. This is where crypto currencies will affect the individuals, as they are worth the same in every country as it is a border free currency.

Cloud, Servers, services

What does it do?

A cloud server is an infrastructure that is either in a physical form or set up virtually, storing resources, software and data available for users who work remotely over a network or the internet (IBM 2019). Cloud servers make it easy for any user to have access to the resources from anywhere and anytime. All organisational or personal resources, information and software are maintained and controlled by the cloud provider servers. Cloud servers work via an internet connection, it allows users to access and download data on any preferred device such as a laptop, mobile phone and tablets. Cloud storage end users can edit, work and upload documents and files at the same time if users are working together on projects (Fastmetrics 2021). This means all users can be working remotely from anywhere in the world which means to some extent physical office sites may no longer be required. For an individual user to have access to Cloud servers they are required to pay and payments differ from provider to provider. With Apple iCloud which most of us are aware of, we can get up to 5GB of free cloud storage. For any other amounts of storage, individual users can upgrade to a higher storage capacity which they are required to make ongoing monthly payments depending on the storage size the user selects. However, other service providers pricing models may be different and require either yearly or quarterly payments. Furthermore, a Cloud server makes business or individual activities much more efficient. For instance, there is Fastmetrics, a cloud phone service that provides a cloud PBX system (Fastmetrics 2021) on its cloud. A benefit of this server is that it gives free video conferencing calls for everyone all around, without using software (Fastmetrics 2021). This means that it saves money for businesses and individuals, whilst at the same time improving productivity and team collaboration.

What is the likely impact?

The most significant impact of cloud servers comes from the cost savings and increased competitiveness of IT services available to individuals and businesses (Cloud Watch Hub 2020). Cloud technology may also effectively improve other benefits for end-users.

Lower computer cost:

It is not essential to have a high-power computer to operate cloud applications. This is because applications operate in the cloud and a device does not require the processing capability or hard disk storage that is required by modern desktop software (Cloud Watch Hub 2020).

Enhanced Performance:

When fewer programs are running simultaneously, using the computer's memory, it will run more effectively. Desktop’s that utilize cloud services may possibly boot and operate efficiently as they have fewer programs and processes loaded into memory (Cloud Watch Hub 2020).

Easy Team collaboration:

Numerous users can collaborate easily on projects, files, software. All resources, software are hosted on the cloud and this can be accessed by anyone who has a stable internet connection (Cloud Watch Hub 2020).

Unlimited storage:

Cloud services provide virtual storage at an unlimited amount. The current hard drive of a computer device is relatively small compared to what a cloud server can offer. However, the only downside is that it is not free and users who require access to large amounts of storage, are required to pay (Cloud Watch Hub 2020).

Device independence:

When a computer user decides to change or upgrade their computer device due to whatever reason, there is no requirement for a specific version of a program for that particular device. At the same time, there is no requirement to save a file in a specific format (Cloud Watch hub 2020).

Availability of different versions:

Doesn’t matter where you access the document from whether it is from home, work or the public library, the user can access any versions of the document including the last edited document (Cloud Watch Hub 2020).

Reduced software costs:

Unlike the pricey software’s out there, cloud computing services can be granted for free. Examples of such software include Google Docs, Dropbox and Apple iCloud which is appropriate for most individuals (Cloud Watch Hub 2020).

Immediate software updates:

Normally Cloud service applications get automatically updated and available as soon as the user logs into the cloud service. Usually, when a user accesses a cloud-based application, they are presented with the latest version and the user is not required to make any updates (Cloud Watch Hub 2020).

How will it affect you?

I believe Cloud servers will have a positive effect on me as a student. As previously mentioned, Cloud servers have many advantages either for storage or computing. Hence, a student like myself can access documents and applications from anywhere simply by having an internet connection. Alternatively, there is also an option on Google Docs where you can open a document with an internet connection and select the option to work offline, that way the user can make edits to the document and sync it Instead of having hard drives to store documents, programs and applications using cloud services are the number one solution. This is the safest method of storage in case of fire and other accidental or deliberate forms of disasters. Hence, reducing fears of loss of work. Cloud servers also provide an easier means of collaboration for IT students such as myself because it allows us to work on group assignments simultaneously without the need to interrupt one another or the document. Furthermore, having virtual servers makes it convenient by allowing students to work remotely which in turn makes students more productive and attentive. Furthermore, RMIT also provides students with access to OneDrive which helps us save disk space on our personal computers. Additionally, with the benefit of automatic updates on software and applications, it gives us students more time to work on assignments instead of having to worry about regularly updating them. Another positive side of Cloud servers is that there are Cloud hosting services that have special discounts for students.

Cybersecurity

What does it do?

Cybersecurity is the practice of defending computers, servers, networks, information and other devices from threats, such as unauthorised access or disclosure of information, attacks from cyber criminals, and maintenance and protection of files and hardware. Cybersecurity can be referred to as information technology security, computer security or electronic information security and can be broken down into subcategories, such as, Network security which is the practise of securing a computer network from potential intruders and attackers. Application security which focuses on the security of softwares and devices. information security which protects privacy of data being held. Operational security is the practice of protecting data assets and individual pieces of data being aggregated. Disaster recovery and business continuity is the practise of a businesses responses towards a cyber attack or other related events that results in the loss of data, how they recover their operations and information, and backup plans the business has to operate with a loss of data. End-user education which focuses on the security of people using the system, teaching them basic security knowledge so they do not break or damage the system accidentally. With technology continuing to be more widespread and broad in use, the amount of people's daily reliance of technology has increased exponentially year by year. Websites and apps are now a requirement for businesses to be successful, some even adopting an online-only presence, as the benefits of reaching such a wide audience are enough to sway them. With a plethora of businesses now accessible to the internet, cybercrime has inevitably grown in frequency and sophistication. Common Cyber-attack targets of cybercriminals include passwords, banking information and personal information. Cyber-attacks are intentional attempts at breaching an information systems integrity and confidentiality, which are the accuracy and completeness of data, and the protection of sensitive information. There are many ways in which cyber-attacks occur, some of which include different types of malware which are softwares made to break or damage user’s computers. The different types of malware are viruses, which are self replicating programs that infect files, Trojans which are disguised as legitimate programs, Spyware which records the actions the user takes on their computer, and Ransomware which blocks access to files unless a ransom is paid. Other cyber-attacks that occur are Man in the middle attacks (MITM) which is when an attacker intercepts communication between two people and information sent between the two can be retrieved by the attacker. SQL injections which is a type of attack that targets vulnerabilities in a database by injecting malicious code into the database. Phishing, which is an attack that targets people by using emails that look like real companies or industries to trick people into revealing sensitive information. Denial of service attack (DOS) is an attack that stops a service from operating by overwhelming the services with traffic, distributed denial of service (DDOS) is the same as DOS but with multiple computers flooding the service with traffic.

What is the likely impact?

The impact of the advancement of cybersecurity has led to an increase in the number of different jobs involving cybersecurity, such jobs include cybersecurity analyst which consists of maintaining technology security and monitoring traffic in a company's computer network. A penetration tester or ethical hacker is where simulated cyber attacks are performed to find flaws and gaps in the security of websites and other systems. A security software developer who develops security software and implements security into applications. Because most if not all people use the internet and with it website and web applications, the majority of people are affected by what happens with cybersecurity, as some cybersecurity experts predict that “in 2021, there will be a cyber attack incident every 11 seconds”. This increase is because of how important information and data is to people. These attacks can be used to stop the services of a company or retrieve data such as a person's credit card number and bank details. Data breaches impact companies and organisations by damaging their reputation and trust between keeping users data safe, and costs the organisation a lot of money to repair damaged systems. Organisations that fail to update their cybersecurity are not only impacted by data breaches caused by cyber attacks but the users themselves who are victims of stolen data are also impacted as their own personal data is exposed and can be exploited. This causes users to have to change passwords if they have the same password for every website they use. If hackers are persistent enough, they can target a weaker service that an individual uses and find out the password that they use and thus gain access to other services the individual uses with the same password even though the service may have stronger cybersecurity.

How will it affect you?

Due to the increase in technology used by people around the world and how this technology is integrated into our daily lives such as social media and work, the effect cybersecurity has on us has increased immensely. With how a majority of our lives are online the importance of having strong cybersecurity increases. The importance of cybersecurity is clear with how without it, a lot if not all of our personal information will be known by everyone. The main implementation of cybersecurity that all of us face in your daily lives is the use of Hypertext Transfer Protocol Secure or HTTPS. HTTPS is the secure version of HTTP and is an extension used to send data between a web browser and a website. However the HTTPS encrypts the information sent through the web browser with the use of two different keys, the private and public key. The Private key is owned and kept secret by the web server and is used to decrypt messages sent, that have been encrypted by users of the website using the public key. The Public key is a key that is available to all people that connect to the web server and encrypts information sent by them. This information can only be decrypted by the web servers Private key. Without the use of HTTPS people with the right tools are able to view information that has been sent through the web browser and website as plain text, while with the use of the protocol they will be able to receive the information but it will be viewed as an encrypted message that they would not be able to decrypt.
